ISO

ISO is a somewhat tricky thing to define. Wikipedia explains it as fallows "For digital photo cameras ("digital still cameras"), an exposure index (EI) rating—commonly called ISO setting—is specified by the manufacturer such that the sRGB image files produced by the camera will have a lightness similar to what would be obtained with film of the same EI rating at the same exposure."
All you really need to know about it though is that you should always use as low of an ISO as possible. The lower the ISO the more light it will need, but lower ISOs will also result is more accurate colours and less static. Only use a high ISO is the really isn't enough light to take the photo at a lower ISO setting.
